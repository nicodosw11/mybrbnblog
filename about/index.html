My first e-commerce

In my spare time I am willing to continue to develop my own marketplace for users who want to learn how to dance (or teach others) and meet lots of people at the same time

On this platform users should be able to follow what events are on (or organise their own meetup), which routines they will learn or practice and book their participation.

2. Build a booking + shopping cart system

Then I wanted to go beyond the usual CRUD applications and started to code a "marketplace" where users share and buy the routines they like, organise or book an event to meet and dance.

It was the best opportunity for me to think about how to build a shopping cart system and how to implement a marketplace given a specific business logic. Users can add routines to their basket, check their final order and pay with Stripe (test mode).

A real app

Building the application I had to go through the whole development process, starting with designing the database, choosing the right models and connections.

After implementing the usual CRUD views and controllers, I had to think through how to articulate both authentication and authorization within the application on top of my own admin layer.

Then I built the administrative back-end to add deal-related information to the database and to publish and manage the listing of opportunities shown to the users.

I also developed the investment interface and presentation of key investment metrics, implemented the back-end for transactions management and calculation of the metrics.

The full GitHub code repository is available here.

3. Build a real-life app: my first MVP (Livsty)

Finally I made it! I have built my first MVP as a freelancer. I met an entrepreneur in London whose idea is to redefine the French viager system by reversing the scheme's cash-flow structure.

Livsty was born. I coded from scratch a crowdfunding platform where investors can pledge for shares in advertised properties. The start-up is now based in Paris, backed by a start-up accelerator and is waiting for more and more investors.
