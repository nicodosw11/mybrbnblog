My way to programming

As new tech changes the way entire industries operate, roles are also adapting and we need to build new skillsets. So I started with a coding bootcamp in Ruby, Rails & JavaScript which acted as a catalyst for my new career. After the bootcamp I felt that the next step to progress from being a Junior Ruby developer was to build a MVP for a start-up as a freelancer. Then I joined Utelier for my very first "commercial experience" as people like to say.

2-year experience as a developer

I am a full-stack Ruby/JavaScript developer with a background in banking and (almost) two years of experience as a developer: one as a freelancer building a MVP for Livsty and now as a developer at Utelier. I am passionate about programming because it is both creative and intellectually stimulating. Still I feel very much interested in finance, which is why ideally I would like to work in an environment where these two worlds come together.

Financial Analyst

BNP Paribas/HSBC/Close Brothers – Credit & Market Risk

I come from a background in finance and I graduated with a master's degree in Banking from Paris Dauphine University. I chose to relocate to London early in my career because London offered the international and multicultural environment I've always been looking for. I worked as a contractor for three years first at the french bank BNP Paribas, where I was in charge of monitoring financial exposure of the bank and ensuring it was correctly reflected in the systems across the whole range of products. Then at HSBC, my role evolved towards interacting with traders and quants and investigating any mismatch between trading and risk systems. That is when I realized that what I was really interested in was to build and improve those systems myself rather than just analysing them and I started to learn programming on my own. When looking for my next contract I was offered a permanent position at Close Brothers and that was the opportunity for me to settle down. The role has proved to be very interesting as I was exposed to high-profile property developments, energy assets financing and even Barclays Premier League football transfers.

Freelance web developer

LIVSTY, London – Ruby, Rails, JavaScript/jQuery, PostgreSQL

Built the Minimum Viable Product for Livsty, a crowdfunding platform where investors can buy shares in advertised properties. Livsty is redefining the French viager system by reversing the scheme's cash-flow structure. Based in Paris, Livsty is an online investment platform which enables everyone to invest in occupied properties via the french Viager scheme and makes accessible to everyone a forward-looking saving scheme both responding to societal challenges and maximising income. Property owners can also sell their properties with Livsty saving them the tiresome advertising, appointments and negotiations.

FullStack web developer

UTELIER, London – Ruby, Rails, JavaScript/Elm, AWS, PostgreSQL

Utelier wants to disrupt the current process of taking a design to a manufacturer which is today incredibly complex and painful. Utelier has been working on a SaaS fashion manufacturing platform for a couple of years, connecting designers and manufacturers globally. The platform is yet to be officially launched but about almost 2000 brands are already effectively using it. Sitting on the same application, the final platform will be three-fold. A Directory acting as a manufacturing database where you can find designers and fashion brands. A specification sheet ("Spec" still in beta) area acting as a blueprint tool for products to facilitate communication between designers and manufacturers and aiming to create a "universal spec", an industry standard, communicating in different languages and conveying ideas easily and accurately. An optional online workshop ("Tender ") & collaborative tool with everything related to production, design to shipment can be managed in one hub.
